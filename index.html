Instructions

Tips

Step One

Decide who will share their screen and draw questions. Determine an order of speaking. We recommend beginning with the person who has done Hello Neighbor the most times and then continuing in order of whomever is closest to their hometown.

Step Two

First person starts with an introduction. Intros could include family, hometown, hobbies, what sparks joy, etc. Continue introductions in the order your group decided.

Step Three

Once introductions are done, draw a question card by clicking on the deck. The first person who introduced themself should answer. Follow-up questions from participants are strongly encouraged.

Step Four

After the first person finishes, draw a new question card. The next person should answer it, continuing in the order of introductions. If you don’t like the question, you can draw another or answer a question previously drawn.

Step Five

Keep drawing cards and answering questions until the time is up. The questions are meant to spark conversation. If something piques the group’s interest, feel free to spend as long as you want on the topic. Enjoy the conversation!

  1. Be present. Actively listen to the current speaker.
  2. After someone finishes sharing, we strongly recommend follow-up questions.
  3. We encourage openness and vulnerability, but it is always your choice. If there is a question you do not like, feel free to answer a previously drawn question or draw another.
  4. Provide equal space for each person to share.
  5. Assume what is said in your small groups should stay in your small groups.
  6. Create any additional ground rules with your small group, if necessary.
